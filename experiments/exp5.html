Experiment 05

Collection of Email Evidence in the Victim's PC

🎯 Aim

To collect, preserve, and analyze email evidence from a victim's computer, including email headers, body content, attachments, and metadata, following proper forensic procedures.

📖 Theory

Email forensics is the study and examination of email content, email headers, and email communication patterns to identify evidence of malicious activity, fraud, harassment, data exfiltration, or other crimes. Emails are one of the most common forms of digital evidence in cybercrime cases.

Email Header Analysis is particularly valuable. Every email carries a header that records the path it took through mail servers. Key header fields include:

  • From / To / CC / BCC — Sender and recipient addresses
  • Date — Timestamp of the email
  • Message-ID — Unique identifier assigned by the sending mail server
  • Received — Chain of mail servers through which the email passed, with timestamps
  • X-Originating-IP — The IP address of the original sender's device
  • MIME-Version / Content-Type — Email encoding and attachment metadata

Email clients such as Microsoft Outlook store messages in PST (Personal Storage Table) or OST (Offline Storage Table) files. Thunderbird uses MBOX format. These files can be analyzed using forensic tools to retrieve all emails, including deleted messages.

🛠️ Tools Required

Autopsy — Used to parse PST/OST files from a forensic image and recover email artifacts.

Aid4Mail / MailXaminer — Dedicated email forensics tools for parsing and reporting on email databases.

MXToolbox Header Analyzer — Web-based email header analysis tool.

Microsoft Outlook / Thunderbird — Email clients whose storage files are analyzed.

📋 Procedure

  1. Locate Email Storage Files

    On the victim's PC (or forensic image), locate email database files. For Outlook, the default paths are:
    C:\Users\[User]\Documents\Outlook Files\*.pst
    C:\Users\[User]\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Outlook\*.ost

  2. Create a Forensic Image (If Not Already Done)

    Use FTK Imager to create a bit-for-bit image of the drive (refer to Experiment 04) before examining email files, ensuring evidence preservation.

  3. Load PST File into Autopsy

    In Autopsy, add the forensic image as a data source. Navigate to Email Messages in the results tree. Autopsy will parse the PST/OST and list all emails.

  4. Examine Email Headers

    Select a suspicious email and view its full raw header. Identify the X-Originating-IP field to trace the sender's IP address.

  5. Trace IP Address

    Use an IP geolocation service or whois to resolve the originating IP to a geographic location and ISP:

    whois 203.0.113.45

  6. Extract Attachments

    In Autopsy, navigate to Attachments under the email module. Extract and hash all attachments. Check file hashes against known malware databases (VirusTotal).

  7. Document and Report

    Export a full email evidence report from Autopsy. Record all findings including sender IP, timestamps, email body excerpts, and attachment hashes in the forensic report.

📝 Observations & Result

Email artifacts were successfully recovered from the PST file using Autopsy. Header analysis revealed the originating IP address, which was geolocated to a specific region. Attachments were extracted and their MD5 hashes were recorded. The complete email thread, including sent and deleted items, was recovered and documented.

Result: Email evidence was successfully collected and analyzed from the victim's PC, demonstrating the process of email forensics including header analysis, IP tracing, and attachment examination.
